# How to Update Your Portfolio

Hi Dylan — this is the short guide to adding new work to your site. You don't need to touch any code.

## Logging in

1. Go to **yourdomain.com/studio** (replace with your actual domain).
2. Log in with the email you used when the site was set up.
3. You'll see a sidebar with three sections: **Site Settings**, **Long-Form Project**, and **Short / Social Post**.

Whenever you make a change, click the green **Publish** button in the top right. Until you publish, the change is only saved as a draft.

After publishing, allow ~1–2 minutes for the live site to update.

## Adding a new long-form project (documentary, branded film, feature)

1. Click **Long-Form Project** in the sidebar.
2. Click the green **+** button (top right) to create a new one.
3. Fill in:
   - **Title** — e.g. "Last Run"
   - **Slug** — click "Generate" to auto-fill it
   - **Short Description** — one or two sentences shown next to the thumbnail
   - **Thumbnail / Cover Image** — upload a 16:9 still (1600×900 looks great)
   - **Role** — what you did (Director, Editor, DP, etc.)
   - **Client / Outlet** — who it was for
   - **Year** — e.g. 2025
   - **Video URL** — paste the YouTube or Vimeo link. The site will embed it automatically.
   - **External Link (optional)** — only if the work lives somewhere else
   - **Featured on Homepage** — turn on for the 3–5 best pieces you want rotating on the homepage
   - **Homepage Order** — leave blank, or use a number (1, 2, 3…) to control order in the rotation
   - **Publish Date** — when it released
4. Click **Publish**.

## Adding a new short / social post

1. Click **Short / Social Post** in the sidebar.
2. Click **+** to create a new one.
3. Fill in:
   - **Caption / Title** — the line shown under the card
   - **Platform** — Instagram, TikTok, or YouTube Shorts
   - **Public Post URL** — paste the full URL of the post
   - **Thumbnail (vertical)** — upload a portrait/9:16 still
   - **Handle / Account** — leave blank to use your default handle
   - **Publish Date** — when it posted
4. Click **Publish**.

The card appears on the **Shorts** page. When a visitor taps it, it plays the social video right on your site (with a fallback link to the original post if the platform changes anything).

## Updating your name, bio, contact, etc.

1. Click **Site Settings** in the sidebar.
2. Edit:
   - **Display Name**
   - **Tagline / Title** (e.g. "Sports Media Director & Cinematographer")
   - **Hero Subcopy** (the short blurb on the homepage)
   - **About Page Photo**
   - **About Bio** (press Enter twice for new paragraphs)
   - **Selected Clients / Outlets**
   - **Contact Email**
   - **Instagram / TikTok / YouTube / LinkedIn**
3. Click **Publish**.

## Tips

- **Image sizes:** thumbnails for long-form work look best at 16:9 (1600×900 or larger). Shorts thumbnails should be vertical 9:16 (720×1280 or larger).
- **Featured projects:** keep this to 3–5. The homepage rotates through them automatically.
- **Video links:** YouTube and Vimeo URLs work for long-form. The Shorts page accepts Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube Shorts URLs.
- **Drafts:** unpublished items don't show up on the live site. Use this to draft something you're not ready to share yet.
- **Deleting:** click the trash icon inside any document. Be sure — there's no undo on the live site.

## If something looks wrong

- Try a hard refresh (Cmd+Shift+R on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows).
- Wait a minute or two after publishing — there's a short delay.
- If a social embed doesn't play, the card will still link to the original post.
- If something is really stuck, contact whoever set up the site.
